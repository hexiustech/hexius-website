Common questions

What's the difference between a pentest and a vulnerability scan?
A scanner identifies known CVEs. A pentest validates whether those CVEs are actually exploitable in your environment, chains vulnerabilities to show real impact, and uncovers business logic flaws that no scanner catches.
Do you test production environments?
Yes, and we prefer it. Production environments surface real configurations, real data flows, and real exposure. We agree on a testing window and rollback protocol before starting.
What happens if you find a critical issue during testing?
We notify you immediately, out of band, before the final report. You decide whether to pause testing, hot-patch, or continue. We never hold critical findings until the end.
How is confidentiality and data handling managed?
We sign an NDA before scoping. All findings, credentials, and test data are stored encrypted and destroyed after report delivery. We never retain client data.
Can your report be used as evidence for SOC 2 or ISO 27001?
Yes. Our reports map findings to relevant control frameworks. Many clients use them directly in their audit evidence packages.

Common questions

What is the difference between a TTX and a red team exercise?
A red team exercise tests your technical defenses by actively attacking your systems. A TTX is a facilitated discussion: your team responds to a simulated breach scenario without actual system compromise. TTX is faster to run, requires no production access, and focuses on decision-making and playbook readiness rather than technical exploitation.
Do participants need technical knowledge to take part?
No. TTX is designed for mixed teams: executives, legal, communications, IT, and security. The value comes from having the right decision-makers in the room, not from technical depth. Facilitators calibrate the scenario complexity to your team's profile during scoping.
How realistic are the scenarios?
Scenarios are built from real APT TTPs relevant to your sector. We map the attack chain to your actual technology stack, vendor relationships, and documented threat landscape, not a generic template. If your sector is Healthcare, the scenario references real threat actors that target Healthcare organizations.
What happens if we don't have an incident response playbook yet?
That is one of the most common situations we see. The TTX will surface exactly what decisions and procedures need to be documented. The deliverable includes playbook creation guidance, so you leave the exercise with a clear starting point, not just a list of gaps.
Can this exercise count toward compliance requirements (SOC 2, ISO 27001)?
Yes. Tabletop exercises are a recognized control activity under SOC 2 (CC7.3, CC7.4) and ISO 27001 (A.16, A.17). Our report documents scenario, participants, decisions made, and gaps identified, the format most auditors accept as evidence.

Common questions

What is the difference between OSINT and a vulnerability scan?
A vulnerability scan probes your systems for known CVEs and misconfigurations. OSINT doesn't touch your systems at all. It collects and analyzes what is publicly visible about your organization: employee data, meta-data, leaked credentials, exposed infrastructure, and dark web mentions. The output is a risk map from an attacker's perspective, not a list of software versions.
Do you need access to our systems or network?
No. OSINT is entirely passive and external. We do not connect to your systems, use credentials, or require any network access. Everything we collect is already publicly available. We find it, correlate it, and translate it into exploitable risk before an attacker does.
What kind of data do you actually find?
Commonly: employee email addresses and LinkedIn profiles (including C-suite and assistants), leaked credentials from past data breaches, forgotten subdomains and staging environments, exposed technology stack details, public documents with embedded metadata, and dark web mentions. Volume and sensitivity varies significantly by organization.
Is this legal?
Yes. OSINT assessment is entirely passive and limited to publicly available information. No unauthorized system access, no hacking, no deception. We collect what is already in the open, the same data an attacker collects during pre-attack reconnaissance. The engagement is documented with written scope authorization before we begin.
How do we use the report to reduce exposure?
The report is organized by category and prioritized by exploitability. Start with the highest-severity people findings: executive exposure is typically the fastest to reduce and the most impactful. Then address leaked credentials and exposed infrastructure. The remediation briefing helps your team prioritize and assign owners while the assessment is still fresh.

Common questions

What is the difference between a vCISO and a security consultant?
A security consultant delivers a scoped assessment or project and exits. A vCISO is an ongoing embedded security leader: responsible for your program, your roadmap, and your risk posture, not just a one-time deliverable. We own the outcomes, not just the engagement.
Do we need to have a security program already in place?
No. We start from where you are. Many clients come to us with no formal security program, no policies, and no compliance baseline. The Discovery phase is designed to assess your current state and build from the ground up if needed.
Can you help us prepare for SOC 2 or ISO 27001?
Yes. Compliance preparation is one of the most common engagement types. We conduct a gap analysis against the target framework (SOC 2 Type I/II, ISO 27001, or Law 25 / Bill 64), build a remediation roadmap, assist with control implementation, and prepare your evidence package for audit.
How does the retainer work: what does a typical month look like?
A typical month includes a strategic check-in (bi-weekly or monthly depending on scope), risk register review, progress tracking against the roadmap, vendor or policy decisions requiring security input, and a board-ready summary if needed. Scope is adjusted based on active initiatives.
What happens if we have an incident during the engagement?
We provide immediate escalation support: helping you activate your IR plan, coordinate with legal and regulatory contacts, and manage the response process. This is included in the retainer scope. For organizations without an IR plan, building one is typically one of the first deliverables.
